If content goes outside the margin or border, you can fix it with the overflow property. Overflow=Auto and Overflow=Scroll puts a scrollbar next to the text and you can slide it to view the rest of the test. Overflow=Hidden hides the overflowing text and the text that can fit stays. Overflow=Visible shows all of the content regardless of wether it goes outside the box.

The overflow property also works for images that have pixels going outside the margin. Overflow=Auto and Overflow=Scroll will make two scroll bars on the right side and on the bottom and you can toggle the scroll bars to view the image. Overflow=Hidden will show whichever part of the image appears in the margin box without going over. THe rest gets cut off. Overflow=Visible will show the whole image, even if it goes outdies the box.

With content area, you can use pixels or percentages. When doing a percent for the text area, the percent will signify what part of the parent tag the width or height will take up. You don't want to use percentages for the height and use pixels instead because you can scroll up and down in a page and the height will automatically change anyways.
